Shop Act License

A ShopAct License, also known as the Gumasta License, is a mandatory permit required by businesses operating in Maharashtra. It is governed under the Maharashtra Shops and Establishments Act and is issued by the local municipal corporation or the labor department.

Factory Act License

As per the Factory Act 1948 any factory having more than 20 workers and using electricity or any factory with more than 40 workers is required to apply for the factory licenese. It takes around 30-45 days to get a complete factory license registration

EPFO and ESIC

PF and ESIC are two different types of social security schemes in India that are designed to benefit employees. While PF is mandatory for all employees earning more than Rs. 15,000 per month, ESIC is mandatory for all employees earning less than Rs. 21,000 per month.

What are the consequences of non-compliance with the Shop Act?

Non-compliance can lead to fines, penalties, and legal action. Regular inspections are conducted to ensure adherence to labor laws and safety standards.

What is the Factories Act, and who does it apply to?

The Factories Act is legislation aimed at ensuring the safety, health, and welfare of workers in factories. It applies to establishments employing 10 or more workers with power and 20 or more workers without power.

How do I register for EPF (Employee Provident Fund)?

To register for EPF, employers must obtain an Establishment Code from the EPFO portal and enroll eligible employees, ensuring regular contributions towards their provident fund accounts.

How can I apply for a health license?

To apply, submit an application to the local municipal corporation with details about your business, along with necessary documents like proof of premises, NOC from the fire department, and sanitation certificates.
